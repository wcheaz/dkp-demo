TECHNICAL REPORT
 
The plumbing project addresses water supply and drainage of sewage and rainwater
 from the proposed family house FAMILY HOUSE  ,,MM,, Jakubov. The basis for the preparation
 of the project were the drawings of the structural part and the investor's requirements.
 When preparing the project documentation for plumbing installations, the following
 standards were mainly used:
STN EN 12056 Gravity drainage systems inside buildings
STN 736760 Internal sewerage
STN 736620 Water supply pipes
STN 736655 Calculation of internal water supplies
STN 736660 Internal water supplies
STN 060803 Safety equipment for central heating and domestic hot water
heating
STN EN 1717 Protection of drinking water against pollution inside buildings and general
requirements for safety devices to prevent pollution from backflow
STN EN ISO 12241 Thermal insulation of building equipment and industrial installations
 
External sewerage.
There is currently no public sewerage on the site in question (WWTP under construction). For
the drainage of the building, a new prefabricated watertight cesspit with a volume of 10m3 will be built.
The cesspit will be installed near the fence and will be equipped with a trafficable (D400) manhole cover
600/600mm. Ventilation of the cesspit will be via the inlet pipe of the sewerage. Pumping out of the cesspit will be 2x per
month, as needed. After the completion of the WWTP, the family house will be connected directly to the public part of the sewerage.
The proposed house will be drained into the newly built cesspit via a sewerage service connection
DN150mm. The sewerage pipe underground, made of PVC socket pipes, will be laid at a slope
in a dry excavation trench on a level, compacted sand bed 150 mm thick. The pipe will be
backfilled to a height of 300 mm above the top of the pipe with sand. The backfill will be evenly compacted in layers 150 mm thick;
directly above the pipe it will not be compacted. In case of groundwater occurrence, the trench will be drained
by drainage or pumping.
Calculation of cesspit volume
 
Assumed number of persons in the building   =4
Average daily quantity of wastewater     = 135 l/day
Interval for pumping out the cesspit contents   15 days
accumulation volume of the cesspit   15 x 0,135 x 4 = 8,1 m3

Rainwater drainage.
Drainage of the built-up area will be via external rainwater downspouts to the terrain, or into barrels for
the purpose of watering greenery.
 
External water supply.
 
External water supply.
 
In the adjacent street there is a public water main DN100, from which an existing
service connection DN32 is led to the water meter pit on the investor's property. The domestic water meter is located in a prefabricated
water meter pit on the investor's property. Access to the pit is secured by an access trafficable
manhole cover (D400) with dimensions 600 mm x 600 mm. In the pit
HDPE 100, PN16 pipes will be used for the service connection, which will be laid at a slope at the bottom of a dry
excavation trench on a compacted, level sand bed 150 mm thick. The pipe will be backfilled with sand to a height of
300 mm above the top of the pipe. The pipe backfill will be evenly compacted in layers 150 mm thick;
directly above the pipe it will not be compacted. The trench will be backfilled with excavated material and the backfill will be compacted in
layers.
 
Water supply material

underground water supply: plastic pipes for drinking water made of HDPE100 PN16
 
 
Water supply testing
After installation of the water supply pipes, flushing, testing and disinfection of the entire distribution system will be carried out according to
STN 73 6660. A record of the tests and water quality will be made.
 
 
Sewerage.
 
Internal sewerage.
Pipe penetrations through the base concrete from the ground into the interior (in the house) must be sealed
against groundwater and at the penetration point there will be a fixed point (see the technological specifications of the pipe manufacturer).
Waste pipes and connection pipes of the sewage will be routed in chases in
the walls or exposed with additional covering. Connection pipes will be laid at a slope of at least 2-
3%. Risers will be wrapped with insulation 0.9mm thick (Mirelon).
Drainage from the washing machine and dishwasher will be discharged through a trap HL406.
All sewerage pipes will be fastened to the building structures with elements with
a rubber lining to prevent noise transmission. Proper function of the gravity sewage will be
ensured by a vent pipe led above the roof and terminated with a plastic vent cap
(e.g. HL810). Cleaning of waste pipes will be possible through cleaning fittings terminated with a threaded
closing cap, which will be placed one meter above the ground floor. All changes in direction
of the sewerage pipes will be assembled with elbows at an angle of maximum 45°.
After completion of the installation of the internal gravity sewerage, tests will be carried out according to
STN 73 6760.

Water supply.
 
 
Internal water supply
The pipe penetration from the ground to the ground floor must be sealed against groundwater and connected to
the waterproofing in coordination with the structural part solution.
Internal water supply pipes will be laid one above another covered by the structure, or routed
in the floor.
Hot water preparation will be provided by an electric boiler. Connection of the boiler to the water supply will be in
accordance with STN 060830 and STN EN1717. Hot water distribution systems will be equipped with forced circulation
with a circulation pump (e.g. Grundfos UP 15-14 BUT).
 
The internal water supply will be vented through the outlet fittings and drained through the lowest
positioned fittings. All pipes will be fastened to the building structures with fastening
elements with a rubber lining to prevent noise transmission. Fittings will be positioned so that they are freely
accessible, inspectable and replaceable. After completion of the installation, a pressure test,
flushing and disinfection of the water supply will be carried out.

synthetic rubber (e.g. Armaflex),


polyethylene (e.g. Tubolit)

General conditions
 
Installation of plumbing systems may only be carried out by an organization that has the
authorization for this activity and trained workers who meet the conditions of professional competence for
carrying out the relevant installation work. A record of the construction and installation work progress shall be
kept in the construction diary.
The construction materials and products used must comply with the conditions of the building act and
the act on construction products. Installation work will be carried out in accordance with the valid technical
standards and technological specifications of the manufacturers of construction materials and products, in compliance
with the valid safety regulations.
During implementation, it is necessary to respect existing underground and above-ground equipment. Before
starting construction work, all existing underground lines must be surveyed by their
administrator. When crossing and running parallel with the proposed pipe and existing networks, the
conditions of STN 736005 must be observed. At the points where the proposed pipe crosses existing lines
and at points where their damage could occur, manual excavation must be carried out.
