Brief description of the site
The mixed-use building will be situated in the village of Veľké Janíkovce on the investor's plot.
The terrain of the plot is slightly sloping.
The plot has a triangular shape and is located between Dlhá and Hlavná streets.
Access to the plot is directly from the road — proposed from the northeast side
from Hlavná street. The entrance to the building itself is designed from the northwest side into
the café and the southwest side into the fitness center. From the northeast side,
an entrance for supply deliveries is designed.

Impact of the building on the environment, fire protection and occupational safety:
The building project complies with all applicable standards and regulations. During construction,
all applicable STN and ON regulations must be followed and all
safety regulations must be observed.
The building will not have a negative impact on the environment of the surrounding development.

focus on:
·         water protection (Act No. 138/73 Coll. On Waters)
·         soil and greenery protection (Act No. 287/94 Coll. On Nature and Landscape Protection).
The construction site is not located in a protected landscape area.
Layout and operational design:
The mixed-use building is situated in the village of Veľké Janíkovce. It is designed as a freestanding
building with two above-ground floors and a set-back floor, which has an area of 49,86%
of the floor below it.
The plot is located directly adjacent to the road, which contains a public
water supply, sewer and electrical lines. Natural gas is not available in this area.
The floor plan of the building has a triangular shape. It will be roofed with a flat roof.
From the northwest side, the entrance to the café is designed through a canopy.
The front part consists of the café itself. On the southwest side, hygiene facilities and
a room for the cleaning staff are situated. On the northeast side, there is the café's back-of-house with an external entrance for
supply deliveries.

Architectural and artistic design:
The mixed-use building fits in with the surrounding development in terms of mass, height and volume.
Its shape is defined by the shape of the plot and the water main protection zone that crosses
the plot. The building has two floors, with a basement and a set-back upper floor. Window
openings are located primarily on the northeast and southwest sides.
The building faces the main street with its longer side. The facade consists of white
exterior plaster by baumit combined with cladding on the insulation. Window and door frames
are anthracite-colored.
Paved surfaces are made of interlocking concrete pavers premac and are directly
connected to the public road. The parking area is designed with grass
pavers.
The building's appearance is accentuated by the proposed surrounding greenery — see separate
vegetation design project.
Structural design
Structurally, the building is designed as masonry using POROTHERM exterior blocks
thickness 300mm with MINERAL WOOL insulation thickness 200mm. Interior partition walls
are POROTHERM thickness 115 and 140mm. 
The roof structure is formed by a trussed rafter. Ceiling - suspended
fire-resistant drywall. The facade consists of exterior plaster by baumit.
Windows and doors are plastic.

TECHNICAL REPORT
Before starting construction work, it is advisable to build temporary construction site facilities
serving to protect workers from adverse weather and for
material storage (cement, lime, tools). Furthermore, it is necessary, according to instructions
from the utility companies, to establish a temporary electrical connection         (220, 380 V) with
a lockable meter box. To protect materials and equipment, it is recommended
to fence the construction site and close it after completion of work.
1. GEOLOGICAL AND HYDROGEOLOGICAL CONDITIONS
Geological conditions were not investigated at the construction site. The investor will arrange
a geological survey before construction to determine the exact foundation method — which the
structural engineer will specify based on the geological survey in the execution project. 
2. EARTHWORKS
Before starting earthworks, the building will be staked out using batter boards. An elevation
benchmark will also be marked, from which all relevant heights are determined. It is necessary to precisely
stake out the routes of existing utility networks to avoid damaging them.
Before starting trench and pit excavation, the top layer of topsoil will be removed to
the required thickness, which will be placed in a stockpile and used for final landscaping.
Excavation work is recommended to be done mechanically; just before concreting the foundations,
manual trimming to the foundation subgrade is necessary. The excavated soil will be kept at
the construction site for subsequent terrain adjustments of the plot and backfilling.
Upon exposing the foundation subgrade, it is necessary to call the structural engineer and assess
the foundation conditions of the subsoil.           If unsuitable foundation conditions are found,
it is necessary to reassess the foundation method of the building. Backfills under structures
must be compacted to a bearing capacity of 0.25 MPa.
3. FOUNDATIONS
Foundation strips with widths from 600 to 1000 mm are monolithic, made of plain concrete
class C16/20. Foundation strip pedestals are concrete made of C16/20 concrete, poured into
DT formwork blocks. Reinforcement of foundation strip pedestals, and possibly also the
foundations, will be specified in the execution project. Foundation footings are 1700x1700 mm,
stepped. Foundations must be below the adjusted terrain at frost-free depth; when founding
on fills, excavation in undisturbed terrain must be at least 300mm. Blinding concrete is
C20/25, reinforced with wire mesh Ф6/150-Ф6/150.  Fill under the blinding concrete
is compacted gravel. During excavation work, it is necessary to assess the soil in the foundation
subgrade and if the soil is less load-bearing than assumed, widen the foundations and reassess
them structurally.
If circumstances are discovered during excavation work that were not accounted for
in the structural engineer's calculation  (e.g., occurrence of fill material, higher groundwater level, etc.),
it is necessary to call the structural engineer or geologist to reassess the foundation method.

4. VERTICAL STRUCTURES
4.1 LOAD-BEARING STRUCTURES
Exterior load-bearing walls of 500 mm thickness are masonry made of ceramic bricks thickness 300
mm, strength P15 (1st floor) and P12  (2nd, 3rd floor), using MVC-5 mortar. Walls are insulated
with insulation thickness 200 mm. Interior load-bearing walls of the building with 250 mm thickness are masonry
made of ceramic bricks of strength P15 (1st floor) and P12 (2nd, 3rd floor), using MVC-5 mortar. Load-bearing walls
are terminated with stiffening ring beams. The structural system is supplemented by an interior
reinforced concrete column 250 x 250 mm and an exterior Ø400 mm column on the terrace. The
structural system of the elevator shaft is a reinforced concrete wall thickness 200 mm made of concrete
class min. C20/25, reinforced with steel B500. Reinforcement according to the structural execution project.
4.1 NON-LOAD-BEARING STRUCTURES ( Partition walls )
Dividing partition walls are designed from precision blocks, thickness 115 and 140mm. Plaster on
masonry must be reinforced with reinforcing mesh to prevent plaster cracking.
5. HORIZONTAL STRUCTURES
5.1 LOAD-BEARING STRUCTURES
Load-bearing walls are terminated with stiffening ring beams of height 250 and 450 mm, which are
continuous above all load-bearing walls. Monolithic ring beams are made of concrete class C20/25,
reinforced with steel B500. Ring beam reinforcement is min. 4Ф12 and 6Ф12, stirrups Ф6 at 250 mm,
reinforcement must be lapped at corners to a splice length of min. 800 mm. The structural system is
supplemented with reinforced concrete beams of height from 500 to 700 mm — under the offset
walls of the upper floor, beams are turned upward. Window lintels are prefabricated,
or reinforced concrete, reinforced according to the structural execution project.
Floor slabs of the 1st and 2nd floors are reinforced concrete continuous slabs of thickness 250 mm, reinforced according to
the structural execution project. Concrete class min. C20/25, steel B500. The staircase is
reinforced concrete, slab type, slab thickness min. 200 mm. The ceiling of the set-back floor is a wooden
truss — forming the roof of the building.
5.2.1. NON-LOAD-BEARING STRUCTURES – FLOORS
The wear layer is designed according to the room's purpose as either ceramic tile
or PVC laminate flooring.
The parking area floor is designed as interlocking pavers or grass
pavers, rated for passenger vehicle loading.

7. OPENINGS
All windows and balcony doors, terrace doors will be plastic, with exterior frame
anthracite-colored with insulating triple clear glass, argon-filled.
The main entrance doors are designed as security doors /with security lock/.
Interior doors and their accessories are standardized. Thresholds are also standardized — according to
the investor's selection.
8. SHEET METAL WORK
Most sheet metal products are standard types; non-standard products and work will be
carried out in compliance with applicable standard requirements.
Flashing of the roof drainage outlet, gutters and downpipes including accessories are made of
copper sheet e.g. thickness 0,7 mm.
Eaves gutters are made from the RANNILA system.
9. INSULATION AGAINST GROUND MOISTURE, ROOF INSULATIONS
Under the roof covering, use insulation from roofing membrane FATRAFOL S 810. 
Against ground moisture, a waterproofing system from PVC membrane FATRAFOL H
803 protected by geotextile is proposed. Insulation is applied to the blinding concrete over the entire
area of the building. The top layer of insulation is extended up the perimeter wall
in the interior to the height of the wear layer. From the exterior side of the building's perimeter walls, extend the insulation 200
mm above the finished ground level.         
In floors with wet use (bathroom, WC), the proposed insulation against
operational water is a layer of 1x HYDROBIT + penetration coating, which must be extended up the
walls to a height of 100 mm (in the shower /bathtub/ area to a height of 150mm). Around the perimeter of the building at
the level under the foundation slab, a linear drainage system is created from perforated PE
pipes surrounded by graded aggregate of fraction 16/32 mm and protected against clogging
by geotextile. In areas where walls contact soil, a surface drainage system using nubbed
PE membrane is used, which also serves as a protective layer for thermal insulation in contact with soil.

CONSTRUCTION MACHINERY
During the construction period, occasional use of the following machinery is anticipated:
crane, excavator, concrete mixer, rotary concrete mixer
for concrete mixes or mortar, attached vibrator and small mechanization of the nature of hand tools.
CONNECTION OF THE CONSTRUCTION SITE TO UTILITY NETWORKS
Electrical energy will be brought to the construction site via a temporary cable connection from
existing utility networks through a construction distribution board as a power source for small construction
machines and tools. The nature of the construction does not require the use of heavy construction equipment
or equipment that would need large electrical power inputs.
Construction water will be provided by a hose connection with subordinate metering
from the existing connection in the adjacent building. Given the type and scope of construction work,
special drainage of the construction site will not be necessary; however, the contractor must
respect the requirements arising from STN 73305.
CONSTRUCTION PROCEDURE
All construction work will be carried out according to prescribed technological
procedures with attention to occupational safety, hygiene and ecology. The concrete mix for
the concrete slab and reinforced concrete ring beam will be processed immediately. Other
prefabricated panels of the steel structure will be prepared off-site and
only installed into the building on site.
